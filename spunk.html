Spunk: a new edition

The project was developed during the editorial design studio at Politecnico di Milano. Each group redesign an album by a band of choice from the 1960s to the 1990s in a contemporary key. We decided to work on a new edition of the bootleg demo album of The Sex Pistols, realized in 1977. The Sex Pistols are an English punk rock band of the Seventies, among the most influential in history and a great musical and aesthetic icons of the first punk wave. Sex Pistols' aesthetic and cultural rebellion is the element that impressed us the most and around which we decided to focus the entire concept of the vinyl.

RE-DESIGNING SEX'S PISTOLS IMAGENERY IN A CONTEMPORARY KEY

The challenge was visually translating the punk rock band's strong imagery into a contemporary key. The band's rebellion was translated through trash aesthetics, using glitches as a defining graphic trait. Indeed, the glitch is seen as a shortcoming of the system. The Sex Pistols were seen as a band against the system as well. The vinyl features an insert inside in which Vivienne Westwood, a famous fashion designer who greatly influenced the band's aesthetic imagery, is portrayed.
