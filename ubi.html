Ubi: exploring the journey

The project was developed during the interaction design studio at Politecnico di Milano, to learn user experience methodologies for digital artefacts. At the end of the course, each group designs a service product system project to solve a need in the parent-child relationship. The group focused on understanding the needs of parents and children during long journeys.

UBI SERVICE

The research phase made it possible to understand the needs of children and parents, which proved to be divergent. Through a series of interviews, the group identified personas, a customer journey for each child-parent pair of personas resulting from the research phase. This phase was necessary to understand a journey for a child: i.e. a slow time to reach the destination. Our challenge was to ask ourselves how we could make the journey itself the experience.

UBI SERVICE

Ubi is the only child travel companion that turns the ride into a fun experience without needing constant parental participation through audio-visual interaction with the external environment. Ubi’s primary goal is to fulfil both the child's and parents' needs, reconciling their different desires, making the trip for the child more stimulating and changing the child’s perspective of the trip. The service comprises three main touchpoints: the website, the app and the telescope. Through the latter, the child can interactively listen to a story, answer some questions and look through the filter.
