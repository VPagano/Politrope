Booktrailer:
Il visconte dimezzato

The project was developed during the Audio Visual Studio at Politecnico di Milano to acquire motion graphic design skills. Each group designed an audiovisual book trailer of "Il Visconte Dimezzato", a short novel by Italo Calvino published in 1952. Through an allegorical-symbolic scenario, the author denounces contemporary man's physical and moral fragmentation, his permanent status of inner contrast between good and evil.

A 18-CENTURY IMAGINARY

The challenge was visually translating a written artefact into a motion graphic one. The ballet metaphor visually translated the book's satirical tone of voice: simple shapes and moves around, creating symmetries and intersections. The warm palette represents the good half, warm colours, and the bad half, the cold one, tells the 18th-century imagination. The colours show the continuous presence of both halves.

THE FOUR STAGES OF LIFE

The book plot is the story of all human beings who, through their personal growth, become aware of the nature of their existence. So, the trailer is divided into four phases that the viscount goes through during his growth: the first phase is characterized by a state of confusion, where the young man believes he knows himself and the world around him; a transitional phase, where he realizes that his truths were nothing but personal beliefs, which are overturned and questioned; the third stage, where the man tries to struggle with himself to try to figure out how he can survive and reach a mature and conscious stage. Then, the conscious stage, where he finally accepts his condition.
